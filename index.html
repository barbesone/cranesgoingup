There are lots of new projects going up around the city that will change the urban environment for decades to come. Right here on Staten Island are some significant developments along the North Shore. Downtown Brooklyn is also the location for important projects, and, of course, Manhattan has a fare share of new construction sites as well.

Here is a list of some new construction projects I would like to focus on in this website:

  • Hudson Yards
  • The World Trade Center
  • 9 DeKalb
  • New York Wheel
  • Governor's Island parkland
  • Empire Outlets
  • Vanderbilt tower

Hudson Yards

By far the largest project going on is the Hudson Yards development and the various smaller construct sites around it. It includes infrastructure upgrades, such as subway line extension and railyard restructuring, as well as buildings, plazas and new streets.

The World Trade Center

This project is well on its way to completion. However, some key elements are still under construction, such as the last office tower and the culture cube.

9 DeKalb

The area around Albee Square in Brooklyn is being redeveloped and one key element will be the new tower behind the Dime Savings Bank building called 9 DeKalb. It will be by far the tallest building in Brooklyn.

The New York Wheel

One unique project on this list is the New York Wheel which will be an attraction drawing thousands of visitors to the North Shore of Staten Island. It is presently under construction in St. George.

Governors Island parkland

The parkland on Governors Island is green and ready for visitors, even though some elements remain to be completed.

Empire Outlets

Taking form right next to the Staten Island ferry terminal are the shops of Empire Outlets. They are part of the redeveoplment plan for the entire North Shore of Staten Island.

Vanderbilt tower

A supertall skyscraper is on the rise next to Grand Central Station and it will change the dynamics at street level in multiple ways.
